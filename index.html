Research

Hamiltonian Causal Models. My main line of work develops Hamiltonian Causal Models (HCMs), a framework in which causal influence leaves a thermodynamic signature. The central idea is that entropy production along a system's trajectories witnesses causal effect: pure gradient dynamics are observationally symmetric, and interventions break that symmetry in a way that identifies causal parents. This yields clean connections between thermodynamic quantities and classical notions like Granger causality and transfer entropy.

Optimal transport, diffusion & geometry. Alongside this, I study the geometry of generative models and of dynamics on distributions — for instance, discrete diffusion on graphs recast as a free-energy gradient flow, and the thermodynamic geometry underlying diffusion-model behaviour. Optimal transport and information geometry are the recurring language throughout.
